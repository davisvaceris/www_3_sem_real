The Market The server hardware market is worth an estimated $55 billion globally, according to Gartner. Milton Research, meanwhile, forecasts a 42 percent decline in overall server shipments in 2017, led by shipments of low-end servers in the services market. At the same time, its research shows an 86 percent drop in revenues for server vendors serving the cloud. In response to the decline, vendors will focus on lower pricing and expanding their direct sales networks to reach consumers, said Antonio Morgese, vice president of market operations for server, storage and networking systems at Dell. "They are trying to build up direct sales for things like storage and networking. They will try to cover all their bases as much as they can," he said. Despite that, Dell saw its overall server shipments rise 13 percent during its fourth quarter, bolstered by a 26 percent rise in storage and 19 percent in networking. Rayner said Dell, which tends to favor storage and networking, was leading the overall server market. He pointed to that and other products, like Nvidia's GPUs, as examples of manufacturers building devices to a range of requirements.
